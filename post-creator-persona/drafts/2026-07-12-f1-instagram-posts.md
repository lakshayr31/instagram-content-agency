## 3) ADUO: F1’s new engine catch-up system, simply explained

**Approved category:** Growth

**Format:** 7-slide carousel

**Why this format:** “ADUO” is technical jargon. A simple explainer carousel converts it into a saveable story: what it is, the 2% rule, and what the first decision means. This is the clearest format for an India-first audience that may not follow power-unit regulations closely.

**Creative concept:** “F1 has a catch-up rule for engines — but it is not a magic speed button.”

**Cover hook:** `F1 HAS A NEW ENGINE “CATCH-UP” RULE`
**Cover subhead:** `ADUO explained in 30 seconds.`

### Slide plan

| Slide | On-screen copy | Visual direction |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **F1 HAS A NEW ENGINE “CATCH-UP” RULE**<br>ADUO explained simply. | Engine graphic + red/yellow/green gap meter. |
| 2 | **What does ADUO mean?**<br>**Additional Development and Upgrade Opportunities.** | Spell out the acronym; use one clean engine icon. |
| 3 | **Why does it exist?**<br>If one engine maker is too far behind, F1 gives it extra chances to improve — so the engine race stays closer. | Three engine-makers on a simple starting line; trailing one gets a “development chance” icon, not a performance boost. |
| 4 | **The 2% rule:**<br>If an engine is **2% to under 4%** behind the benchmark, its maker can get **1 extra upgrade** this season + **1 next season**. | Very simple “2%–3.99%” band → “1 + 1 upgrades” card. |
| 5 | **The 4% rule:**<br>If it is **4%+** behind, it can get **2 upgrades** this season + **2 next season**. | “4%+” band → “2 + 2 upgrades” card. |
| 6 | **Important detail:**<br>The FIA’s ADUO test looks at the **ICE** — the combustion engine — not the full power unit. Energy recovery and battery advantages are outside this calculation. | Split graphic: “ICE = counted” / “ERS + battery = not counted here.” |
| 7 | **What happened first?**<br>The Race reports Red Bull was judged the benchmark. Mercedes gets one extra 2026 upgrade; Ferrari and Honda get two.<br>**That does not guarantee they become fastest overall.** | Red Bull engine at benchmark; Mercedes gets a “1 upgrade” tag; Ferrari and Honda get “2 upgrades” tags; a bold “NOT an instant speed boost” footer. |

### Caption

F1’s 2026 engine rules have a new phrase: **ADUO**.

It means **Additional Development and Upgrade Opportunities** — a rule designed to help engine manufacturers that are genuinely behind catch up through extra development chances.

The simple version:
- **2% to under 4% behind?** One extra upgrade this season and one next season.
- **4% or more behind?** Two this season and two next season.

But here is the key detail: this is based on the **internal combustion engine (ICE)**, not the whole power unit. It does not measure every advantage from energy recovery, deployment, batteries, or the MGU-K.

The Race reports the first decision put Red Bull on top under this ADUO measure, making Mercedes eligible for one extra 2026 upgrade and Ferrari and Honda eligible for two.

So: this is a catch-up mechanism, not an instant “make my car fastest” button.
